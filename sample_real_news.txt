Trump announces US naval blockade of Iran after peace talks fail

US President Donald Trump announced a naval blockade of Iran effective immediately, directing the military to seek and interdict any vessel that paid Iran's toll to cross the Strait of Hormuz. The blockade on traffic entering and leaving Iranian ports began at 10 a.m. Eastern Time on Monday, April 13, 2026, after weekend ceasefire talks in Islamabad, Pakistan collapsed.

Vice President JD Vance, who led the American delegation during the three rounds of talks, said the two sides failed to reach an agreement after fourteen hours of negotiations, citing persistent disagreement over Iran's nuclear weapons development program. The talks had been mediated by Pakistani officials and attended by representatives from the International Atomic Energy Agency.

The economic impact of the escalation has been significant. Brent crude, the global oil benchmark, has risen approximately 40 percent since the conflict began forty-five days ago, with prices approaching 100 dollars a barrel on Monday morning. The International Monetary Fund has revised its global growth forecast downward to 3.1 percent for 2026, citing energy supply disruptions and rising inflation across importing economies.

In response to the blockade, Saudi Arabia announced the restoration of its East-West Crude Oil Pipeline to ease pressure on global supply. European Union foreign ministers convened an emergency session in Brussels to discuss diplomatic alternatives, while China and Russia jointly called for an immediate return to negotiations through the United Nations Security Council.

The United States Navy confirmed that the Fifth Fleet, based in Bahrain, will enforce the blockade with support from carrier strike groups in the Arabian Sea. Pentagon spokesperson Major General Patrick Ryder said in a briefing that the operation is designed to be non-lethal where possible, with vessels given clear warnings before any interdiction. Humanitarian shipments, including medical supplies and food, will be permitted to transit under inspection.
